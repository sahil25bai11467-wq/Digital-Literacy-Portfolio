## What is UPI Payment Fraud?

Unified Payments Interface (UPI) fraud is a form of financial cybercrime
in which fraudsters trick victims into transferring money or approving
payment requests through UPI-based apps such as Google Pay, PhonePe,
Paytm, or BHIM. India's rapid adoption of digital payments has made
UPI fraud one of the most prevalent cybercrimes in the country.

---

## A Realistic Case Scenario

**Background:** Riya, a first-year engineering student, listed her
old laptop for sale on a popular second-hand goods website. She
received a call from someone claiming to be an army officer
interested in buying the laptop immediately.

**Step-by-step: How the fraud unfolded**

1. The caller said he would pay via UPI and asked Riya to share her
   UPI ID. She shared it without hesitation.

2. He then sent a UPI collect request for Rs. 15,000 — the agreed
   price — along with a message saying "Accept this request to
   receive your payment."

3. Trusting this, Riya entered her UPI PIN to "accept" the request.
   Entering the PIN on a collect request actually AUTHORISED a
   DEBIT, not a credit, from her account.

4. Rs. 15,000 was immediately debited. When Riya called back, the
   number was switched off.

**Who is typically targeted?** Young adults, students, and people
selling items on online marketplaces or classifieds.

**Consequences:** Financial loss, emotional distress, and difficulty
in recovering funds. In most cases, UPI transactions are irreversible
and recovery depends entirely on police investigation.

**Key lesson:** On UPI, you NEVER need to enter your PIN to receive
money. A collect request requires your PIN to send money — not to
receive it. If someone tells you to enter your PIN to receive a
payment, it is fraud.
