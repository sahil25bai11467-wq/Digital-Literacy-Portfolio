# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
### CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Sahil Chouksey | 25BAI11467

---

## ✅ DO's

1. **Keep your profiles professional** – Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a bio that reflects your academic and professional interests.

2. **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, future employer, or family saw this?" If the answer is no, don't post it.

3. **Use privacy settings** – Regularly review and update the privacy settings on all your social media accounts to control who can see your content.

4. **Give credit** – Always credit the original creator when sharing someone else's work, ideas, or images.

5. **Engage respectfully** – Disagree with ideas, not people. Keep discussions constructive and polite, even on controversial topics.

6. **Separate personal and professional accounts** – Consider maintaining a professional LinkedIn/GitHub presence separately from casual social media use.

7. **Report harmful content** – If you encounter bullying, hate speech, or misinformation, use platform tools to report it rather than engaging.

8. **Log out on shared devices** – Always log out of your accounts when using public computers or someone else's device.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't share personal information publicly** – Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID number, or daily schedule on public profiles.

2. **Don't engage with or spread misinformation** – Verify information from a reliable source before sharing. Spreading fake news, even unintentionally, has real consequences.

3. **Don't post in anger or frustration** – Emotional posts made in the heat of the moment are hard to take back and can damage your reputation.

4. **Don't accept connection requests from strangers** – Be cautious about who you connect with, especially on platforms where you share personal or professional details.

5. **Don't plagiarise or repost without permission** – Copying someone's content without credit is both unethical and, in many cases, illegal.

6. **Don't overshare your location** – Avoid tagging your real-time location or posting about extended travel plans publicly.

7. **Don't use social media to harass or bully** – Cyberbullying is a criminal offence under the Information Technology Act, 2000 in India.

8. **Don't click on suspicious links in DMs** – Phishing attempts frequently arrive through social media direct messages, even from accounts that appear to be friends.
