## General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. Use a mix of
   uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
   Never reuse the same password across multiple platforms.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important
   accounts — email, social media, banking apps, and UPI apps.
   This adds a critical second layer of protection.

3. **Never click on unknown links** received via SMS, WhatsApp,
   email, or social media — even if they appear to come from a
   known contact. Verify directly with the sender before opening.

4. **Keep your apps and operating system updated** regularly.
   Security patches in updates protect against known vulnerabilities
   exploited by cybercriminals.

5. **Use only official app stores** (Google Play Store or Apple App
   Store) to download UPI and banking apps. Never install APK files
   sent by strangers.

6. **Do not share OTPs, PINs, or passwords** with anyone — including
   people claiming to be bank employees, customer care agents,
   or government officials. Legitimate organisations NEVER ask
   for these details.

## UPI and Financial Safety (Specific to College Students in India)

7. **Remember: You NEVER need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money.**
   If anyone tells you to enter your PIN to receive a payment or
   "accept" a request, it is a scam. Entering your PIN approves
   a debit from your account, not a credit.

8. **Verify QR codes before scanning.** A QR code shared by a
   stranger for a "refund" or "payment" can trigger a collect
   request that drains your account. Only scan QR codes from
   verified merchants or known sources.

9. **Set a daily UPI transaction limit** through your bank's
   mobile banking app. This caps potential losses in case of
   a compromised device or account.

10. **Never respond to fake customer care numbers** found in Google
    searches. Always find the helpline number on the bank's or
    app's official website. Fraudsters create fake websites and
    pose as customer care to steal your credentials.

## If You Are a Victim – Report Immediately

- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** https://cybercrime.gov.in
  (File a complaint online for any cybercrime including UPI fraud,
  phishing, cyberbullying, and identity theft)

- **Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930**
  (Available 24x7, Government of India – report financial cyber fraud
  immediately to freeze funds before they are withdrawn)

- **Also report to your bank** within 24 hours of any fraudulent
  transaction to increase the chance of fund recovery.
